Hedy Lamarr

Engineering

Notable Achievements

  • invented the frequency-hopping spread spectrum
  • inducted into the National Inventors Hall of Fame in 2014
  • received the Electronic Frontier Foundation Pioneer award in 1997
  • has a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame

Hedwig Eva Maria Kiesler was born in 1914 in Austria. She was privately tutored from the age of 4. In addition to her technological contributions, she had a well-known career in film.

During World War II, she submitted her idea to prevent signal-jamming of radio guided torpedoes to the National Inventor's Council. This technology was used to control torpedoes and communication, and is the foundation for bluetooth, wifi, and radio communication in use today. Her idea, developed with composer George Anthiel, was to change the radio signal using the same technology a player piano uses to change notes. If both the transmitter and reciever had the same pre-defined sequence of frequencies to follow, the two would stay in sync while being much harder to track by outside devices that did not know the sequence. This prevented eavesdropping, signal jamming, and reduced noise.

Hedy Lamarr died in 2000 in the United States.
